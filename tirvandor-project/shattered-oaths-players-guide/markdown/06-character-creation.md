## CHARACTER CREATION GUIDE

### STEP 1: CHOOSE YOUR ARCHETYPE
The prophecy mentions seven roles. Pick one that fits your concept:
THE WARRIOR
"Strength in battle, courage in face of tyranny"
Classes: Fighter, Barbarian, Paladin, Ranger
Role: Front-line hero, defender
Theme: Martial prowess, protection
THE SCHOLAR
"Knowledge and wisdom light the path"
Classes: Wizard, Bard, Cleric, Artificer
Role: Lorekeeper, strategist
Theme: Intelligence, planning, history
THE HEALER
"Mercy and compassion in dark times"
Classes: Cleric, Druid, Bard, Paladin
Role: Support, hope-bringer
Theme: Healing, life, renewal
THE ROGUE
"Shadows and secrets, unseen blade"
Classes: Rogue, Ranger, Monk
Role: Scout, infiltrator, spy
Theme: Stealth, information, precision
THE MYSTIC
"Power from beyond, magic unbound"
Classes: Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard, Druid
Role: Raw magical power
Theme: Mystical might, otherworldly
THE GUARDIAN
"Shield for the weak, wall against evil"
Classes: Paladin, Fighter, Cleric, Monk
Role: Protector, defender
Theme: Duty, protection, sacrifice
THE VOICE
"Speaker for the voiceless, inspirer of hope"
Classes: Bard, Cleric, Paladin
Role: Leader, inspirer
Theme: Charisma, hope, unity
Note: These are narrative roles, not mechanical requirements. Two Warriors are fine!

### STEP 2: SELECT RACE
All races work, but consider these perspectives:
HIGHLY THEMATIC:
Human - Most common in both nations, can be from anywhere
Half-Elf - Often caught between worlds, symbolizes unity
Tiefling - Persecuted in Thaldros, fighting back
Half-Orc - Seeking to prove worth beyond stereotypes
STRONG CHOICES:
Elf - Ancient wisdom, connection to old magic
Dwarf - Stubborn resistance, ancestral strength
Dragonborn - Noble bearing, draconic destiny
Halfling - Underestimated heroes, common folk connection
INTERESTING OPTIONS:
Gnome - Clever innovation, unexpected heroes
Cultural Considerations:
From Thaldros:
Defector or escapee
Hunted by your former nation
Know the enemy intimately
Seeking redemption for past
From Aethoria:
Born to the cause
Revolutionary fervor
Deep belief in freedom
Community ties
From the Border:
Neutral turned hero
Tired of the conflict
Wants better future
Outside perspective

### STEP 5: YOUR CALL TO HEROISM
Why did you join this fight?
PERSONAL REASONS:
Loss - Thaldros took someone you love
Injustice - You witnessed/experienced tyranny
Escape - You fled Thaldros oppression
Protection - Defending your community
Revenge - Against specific Thaldros agents (be careful)
IDEALISTIC REASONS:
Belief - Freedom is worth fighting for
Destiny - You believe in the prophecy
Duty - Your gods/ancestors call you
Hope - You want a better world
Justice - Tyrants must be opposed
PRACTICAL REASONS:
Recruited - Aethoria resistance found you
Survival - Safest path forward
Skills - Your abilities are needed
Opportunity - Chance for something greater
Forced - Circumstances left no choice
MYSTICAL REASONS:
Prophecy - You believe you're one of the Seven
Vision - Dreams/visions guide you
Divine Calling - The Ascended chose you
Magical Awakening - Power manifested in crisis
Ancestral - Continuing family destiny

### STEP 6: PARTY BONDS
You're heroes together. How did you meet?
Choose or roll 1d10:
1. Childhood Friends - Grew up together before the conflict
2. Saved Each Other - Met during escape/rescue
3. Shared Vision - All dreamed of the same prophecy
4. Temple Gathering - Met at shrine of the Seven
5. Resistance Cell - Recruited into same rebel group
6. Prison Break - Escaped Thaldros prison together
7. Mentor's Students - Trained by same wise teacher
8. Sole Survivors - Only ones who survived Thaldros attack
9. Prophecy Readers - Scholar identified you as "the Seven"
10. Strangers with Purpose - Just met, but destiny united you
Core Truth: You trust each other. Heroes don't betray heroes.
