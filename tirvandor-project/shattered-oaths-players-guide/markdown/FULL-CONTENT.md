# SHATTERED OATHS: PLAYER'S GUIDE

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

### WHAT IS SHATTERED OATHS?
Shattered Oaths is an epic fantasy campaign about heroes rising to fight tyranny, fulfill an ancient prophecy, and potentially reshape the fate of nations. You play freedom fighters, chosen heroes, or those thrust into greatness, standing against the oppressive might of the Thaldros Empire and championing the cause of liberty.
Core Themes:
Heroism and destiny
Freedom vs. tyranny
Ancient prophecy and fate
Sacrifice and duty
Hope in dark times
Found family and bonds
Campaign Tone:
Epic and inspirational
Morally clear (mostly)
Character-driven drama
High stakes and importance
Medium-high combat, high roleplay, medium exploration

## WHO YOU ARE

### THE CHOSEN (OR THE THRUST-UPON)
You are one of:
THE IDEALIST - You believe in freedom and fight for it willingly
THE RELUCTANT HERO - Circumstances forced you into this, but you'll see it through
THE SEEKING REDEMPTION - Your past haunts you; this is your chance to be better
THE DESTINED - Prophecy speaks of you (or so some believe)
THE LOYALIST - You fight for people/cause you love
What Unites You:
Shared cause (freedom from tyranny)
Belief that things can be better
Willingness to sacrifice for others
Opposition to Thaldros oppression
Destiny (or the appearance thereof)

## THE PROPHECY

### THE SHATTERED OATH
An ancient prophecy, found in the Temple of Moira the Weaver:
> "When crowns grow heavy with the weight of chains,
> When oaths are broken and freedom wanes,
> Seven shall rise where seven once fell,
> To break the cycle, to ring freedom's bell.
>
> Warrior, Scholar, Healer, Rogue,
> Mystic, Guardian, and Voice of the Folk,
> United by purpose, divided by past,
> The Shattered Oath mended at last.
>
> When the Iron Crown cracks at last,
> When the old order's chains are cast,
> Seven souls shall light the way,
> And freedom's dawn shall break the day."
What This Means:
Some believe you're the "Seven" from the prophecy
Thaldros fears this prophecy (they hunt "prophesied ones")
Aethoria sees it as hope
Whether it's real or self-fulfilling is up to you
Prophecy gives you narrative importance but not invincibility

## CHARACTER CREATION GUIDE

### STEP 1: CHOOSE YOUR ARCHETYPE
The prophecy mentions seven roles. Pick one that fits your concept:
THE WARRIOR
"Strength in battle, courage in face of tyranny"
Classes: Fighter, Barbarian, Paladin, Ranger
Role: Front-line hero, defender
Theme: Martial prowess, protection
THE SCHOLAR
"Knowledge and wisdom light the path"
Classes: Wizard, Bard, Cleric, Artificer
Role: Lorekeeper, strategist
Theme: Intelligence, planning, history
THE HEALER
"Mercy and compassion in dark times"
Classes: Cleric, Druid, Bard, Paladin
Role: Support, hope-bringer
Theme: Healing, life, renewal
THE ROGUE
"Shadows and secrets, unseen blade"
Classes: Rogue, Ranger, Monk
Role: Scout, infiltrator, spy
Theme: Stealth, information, precision
THE MYSTIC
"Power from beyond, magic unbound"
Classes: Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard, Druid
Role: Raw magical power
Theme: Mystical might, otherworldly
THE GUARDIAN
"Shield for the weak, wall against evil"
Classes: Paladin, Fighter, Cleric, Monk
Role: Protector, defender
Theme: Duty, protection, sacrifice
THE VOICE
"Speaker for the voiceless, inspirer of hope"
Classes: Bard, Cleric, Paladin
Role: Leader, inspirer
Theme: Charisma, hope, unity
Note: These are narrative roles, not mechanical requirements. Two Warriors are fine!

### STEP 2: SELECT RACE
All races work, but consider these perspectives:
HIGHLY THEMATIC:
Human - Most common in both nations, can be from anywhere
Half-Elf - Often caught between worlds, symbolizes unity
Tiefling - Persecuted in Thaldros, fighting back
Half-Orc - Seeking to prove worth beyond stereotypes
STRONG CHOICES:
Elf - Ancient wisdom, connection to old magic
Dwarf - Stubborn resistance, ancestral strength
Dragonborn - Noble bearing, draconic destiny
Halfling - Underestimated heroes, common folk connection
INTERESTING OPTIONS:
Gnome - Clever innovation, unexpected heroes
Cultural Considerations:
From Thaldros:
Defector or escapee
Hunted by your former nation
Know the enemy intimately
Seeking redemption for past
From Aethoria:
Born to the cause
Revolutionary fervor
Deep belief in freedom
Community ties
From the Border:
Neutral turned hero
Tired of the conflict
Wants better future
Outside perspective

### STEP 5: YOUR CALL TO HEROISM
Why did you join this fight?
PERSONAL REASONS:
Loss - Thaldros took someone you love
Injustice - You witnessed/experienced tyranny
Escape - You fled Thaldros oppression
Protection - Defending your community
Revenge - Against specific Thaldros agents (be careful)
IDEALISTIC REASONS:

Duty - Your gods/ancestors call you

MYSTICAL REASONS:
Prophecy - You believe you're one of the Seven

Magical Awakening - Power manifested in crisis
Ancestral - Continuing family destiny

### STEP 6: PARTY BONDS
You're heroes together. How did you meet?
Choose or roll 1d10:
1. Childhood Friends - Grew up together before the conflict
2. Saved Each Other - Met during escape/rescue
3. Shared Vision - All dreamed of the same prophecy
4. Temple Gathering - Met at shrine of the Seven
5. Resistance Cell - Recruited into same rebel group
6. Prison Break - Escaped Thaldros prison together
7. Mentor's Students - Trained by same wise teacher
8. Sole Survivors - Only ones who survived Thaldros attack
9. Prophecy Readers - Scholar identified you as "the Seven"
10. Strangers with Purpose - Just met, but destiny united you
Core Truth: You trust each other. Heroes don't betray heroes.

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### WHAT TO EXPECT
CAMPAIGN PROGRESSION:
ACT 1: SPARK (Levels 1-5)
Meet, form bonds, discover prophecy
Small-scale resistance operations
Build reputation as heroes
Ends with: Major Thaldros threat/victory
ACT 2: FLAME (Levels 6-10)
Larger operations, factional involvement
Prophecy becomes known (you're hunted)
Difficult choices and sacrifices
Ends with: Major defeat or revelation
ACT 3: INFERNO (Levels 11-15)
War comes to a head
Face greatest Thaldros champions
Fulfill or defy prophecy
Ends with: Epic climax, fate of nations
TYPICAL SESSION:
1. Downtime - Rest, bond, prepare (20 min)
2. New Information - Threats, opportunities, prophecy clues (20 min)
3. Planning - Decide approach, prepare (20 min)
4. The Mission - Heroic action! (1.5-2 hours)
5. Aftermath - Consequences, character moments (30 min)
Combat Frequency: MEDIUM-HIGH
Meaningful battles, not constant
Boss fights are epic
Combat has narrative weight
Roleplay Opportunities: HIGH
Party dynamics and drama
Moral dilemmas (but clearer than Blood & Coin)
NPC relationships and romance
Character growth and arcs
Exploration: MEDIUM
Discover ancient sites
Explore prophetic locations
Journey across Tirvandor

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Q: Do I have to believe in the prophecy?
A: No! Skeptical characters are valid. It's about the journey.
Q: Can we lose?
A: Yes, but not easily. Heroic stories have stakes.
Q: What if my character dies?
A: We'll discuss. Resurrection possible, or heroic sacrifice, or new character.
Q: Can we be dark and edgy?
A: Some darkness is fine, but remember the tone. You're heroes, not antiheroes.
Q: Will romance be in the campaign?
A: If you want! NPC romances are possible. Inter-party romance is up to you (communicate!).
Q: Can I be from Thaldros?
A: Yes! Defectors are great. You know the enemy intimately.
Q: How powerful will we become?
A: Very. By level 15, you're legendary heroes.
Q: Is the prophecy railroading?
A: No. It's a framework, not a script. You have choices.
Q: What if we disagree on morality?
A: Discuss in-character. Heroes can disagree and grow.
Q: Can we change the world?
A: That's the point! Your actions matter greatly.
