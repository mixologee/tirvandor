## The Morally Grey Campaign - Levels 1-5

**Campaign:** Blood & Coin

**Level Range:** 1-5  
**Duration:** 15-20 sessions  
**Tone:** Gritty, morally grey, mercenary survival

## Act I Overview

### **The Premise**

You're not heroes. You're professionals. Sellswords. Problem-solvers for hire. You've come to **Crossroads**, a lawless mercenary town in the frontier regions of Thaldros, where the Crown's authority barely reaches, where the only laws are:

1. **Contracts are sacred** - Break your word, die
2. **Don't kill clients before they pay** - Bad for business
3. **Survive first, moralize later** - Idealism gets you killed

Welcome to the profession. You'll learn quickly that every job has a catch, every client lies, and coin is the only thing you can trustâ€”until you can't.

### **What Act I Teaches**

By the end of Act I, players will understand:
- There are no clear good guys or bad guys
- Every choice has consequences
- Money matters, survival matters more
- Reputation opens doors (and closes them)
- You can't please everyone
- Sometimes the "right" choice gets you killed

### **Starting Setup**

**Location:** Crossroads (pop. 2,400) - neutral mercenary town  
**Starting Level:** 1  
**Starting Gold:** 50gp each (more than standard - you're professionals)  
**PC Backgrounds:** All should have reason to be mercenaries (exiled, desperate, pragmatic, etc.)

## Crossroads - The Mercenary Town

### **Overview**

Crossroads sits at the intersection of three trade routes, far from royal oversight. No official governmentâ€”the **Iron Guild** runs things through contracts and reputation. Violence is common but regulated. Murder is allowed if you have a contract. Theft is punishable by death (bad for business). Lying is expected, getting caught is fatal.

**Population:** ~2,400 (fluctuates with seasonal campaigns)  
**Demographics:** 60% human, 15% dwarf, 10% half-orc, 10% halfling, 5% other  
**Government:** Iron Guild (mercenary guild)  
**Economy:** Mercenary work, black market, vice  
**Alignment:** True Neutral (officially), Chaotic Neutral (practically)

### **Key Locations**

**1. The Iron Guild Hall**
- Three-story stone building, fortified
- Contract Board (public jobs)
- Varak's Office (private contracts)
- Meeting rooms, barracks for members
- Training yard

**2. The Copper Dragon Inn**
- Largest inn/tavern in town
- Run by Mira Copperstone (information broker)
- 20 rooms (5sp-5gp/night)
- Common room always busy
- Best place for rumors

**3. The Grey Market**
- Open-air black market
- Anything can be bought/sold (no questions)
- Weapons, armor, poisons, information
- Fences operate openly here

**4. The Pit**
- Fighting arena
- Legal blood sport
- Betting, recruiting, entertainment
- Reputation-building opportunity

**5. Temple of the Coin**
- Ironic "temple" to wealth
- Actually just gambling hall
- Loan sharks operate from back rooms
- Some healing available (expensive)

### **Factions in Crossroads**

**The Iron Guild** (Dominant)
- Leader: Varak Ironhand (dwarf veteran)
- Philosophy: Contracts sacred, honor in professionalism
- Members: ~400 active mercenaries
- Dues: 10% of contract earnings
- Benefits: Protection, lodging, contracts, reputation

**The Black Chains** (Rival)
- Leader: Captain Kael Darkwater (human mercenary)
- Philosophy: Efficiency over honor, strength over tradition
- Members: ~200 mercenaries (growing)
- Dues: 15% but higher-paying contracts
- Benefits: Better pay, fewer rules, more dangerous

**The Grey Merchants** (Economic)
- Leader: Various merchants, no central authority
- Philosophy: Profit above all
- Control: Black market, trade, vice
- Neutral to guild politics (profit from both)

**Unaffiliated Mercenaries** (Independent)
- No guild membership
- Lower pay, less protection
- More freedom, more risk
- Where most start

### **The Contract Board**

Three contracts are available. PCs must choose ONE (or work together on one).

**Option A: Rat Problem** (Straightforward... at first)

**Option C: Caravan Guard** (Hidden complications)
- **Client:** Merchant Cassia Valen

- **Payment:** 60gp total (split among party)
- **Details:** "Simple escort duty. Leave at dawn, arrive by dusk. Standard bandit territory. Nothing special."

**DM NOTE:** Let PCs read all three contracts. They can discuss, debate, and choose based on their characters' motivations. This choice reveals their priorities: profit (highest pay), morality (least harmful), or pragmatism (easiest job).

## Quest 1.3 - DM Guidance

### **Design Philosophy**

This quest is about **moral complexity in systems**:

**The Dilemma:**
- Elara is legitimately wronged (fraud victim)
- But she's also committed crimes (robbery, assault)
- Consortium legally owns the land (corrupt process, but legal)
- But their ownership is based on fraud
- **Who's right? Who's wrong? Both? Neither?**

**The Test:**
- Will PCs follow contracts blindly?
- Will they break contracts for "righteousness"?
- Can they find middle ground?
- Are they mercenaries or heroes?

### **Key DMing Points**

**1. Present Both Sides Fairly**

Don't make Consortium cartoonishly evil or Elara saintly:
- Consortium genuinely believes business is business
- Elara has killed people (guards who fought back)
- Both have blood on their hands
- Both have valid arguments

**2. Respect All Choices**

Every option in this quest is valid:
- Killing Elara for profit? Valid (mercenary thinking)
- Helping Elara? Valid (justice thinking)
- Staying neutral? Valid (self-preservation)
- Getting creative? Valid (problem-solving)

**3. Consequences Matter**

Whatever PCs choose has lasting effects:
- Break contract? Guild suspends you
- Kill Elara? Villagers remember
- Help Elara? Merchants remember
- Play both sides? Everyone remembers

**4. No Correct Answer**

This quest has no "right" choice, only consequences:
- Players should debate this
- Characters should have different opinions
- Party might split on decision
- That's the point

### **Difficulty Adjustments**

**If Party Struggling:**
- Reduce Elara's follower count (15 instead of 25)
- Give more obvious clues about the fraud
- Make Consortium more obviously evil
- Offer easier Persuasion DCs

**If Party Dominating:**
- Add more followers to Elara's force
- Make Consortium more sympathetic (they didn't know about fraud)
- Add time pressure (Consortium sends second mercenary team)
- Make consequences more severe

### **Roleplaying Elara**

Elara should be **complex, not simple**:

**She's not a victim:**
- She chose this path
- She's killed people
- She's a rebel, not a martyr

**She's not a villain:**
- She's fighting for justice
- She protects her people
- She's willing to talk before fighting

**She's human:**
- Tired of running
- Wants her home back
- Knows she might lose
- Appreciates honesty

**Key Dialogue Beats:**

*"I'm not asking you to be heroes. I'm asking you to not be murderers."*

*"You think I wanted this? I had a castle. A title. A future. The Consortium took that. So I took it back, one caravan at a time."*

*"Kill me if you want. But know what you're doingâ€”you're helping rich men stay rich by killing the people they stole from."*

### **Common Player Questions**

**"Is there a legal solution?"**
Yes, but very difficult (DC 18+ checks, weeks of time, corrupt officials)

**"Can we blackmail the Consortium with evidence?"**
Absolutely! Creative solution rewarded.

**"What if we just take the evidence and sell it to highest bidder?"**
Chaotic Neutral optionâ€”both sides might pay to suppress it.

**"Can we take over the territory ourselves?"**
Technically yes, but requires army/resources PCs don't have at level 3.

### **This Quest Tests:**

- **Contract vs. Conscience** - What wins?
- **Profit vs. Principles** - Which matters more?
- **System vs. Justice** - Can legal be wrong?
- **Pragmatism vs. Idealism** - Where's the line?
