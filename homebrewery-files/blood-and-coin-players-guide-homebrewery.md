# BLOOD & COIN: PLAYER'S GUIDE

## CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

### WHAT IS BLOOD & COIN?
Blood & Coin is a mercenary-focused campaign set in the Border region of Thaldros‚Äîa contested frontier zone between the Kingdom's central authority and independent territories. You play professional fighters-for-hire working for the Iron Guild, taking contracts from anyone with gold to spend. This is a morally gray campaign where money talks, loyalty is negotiable, and survival depends on your skills and reputation.
Core Themes:
Professional mercenary work
Moral ambiguity and gray choices
Reputation and consequences
Gold and contracts over idealism
Tactical combat and strategic thinking
Inter-party dynamics and trust
Campaign Tone:
Gritty and pragmatic
Dark humor and gallows wit
Action-focused with intrigue
Morally complex situations
High combat, medium role play, medium exploration

## WHO YOU ARE

### THE IRON GUILD
You are members of (or affiliated with) the Iron Guild, the largest and most respected mercenary organization in the Border. The Guild operates under strict professional standards:
The Guild Code:
1. Honor Contracts ‚Äì A contract, once signed, is sacred
2. No Betrayal ‚Äì Never betray your squad or employer (during the contract)
3. Fair Payment ‚Äì Good work deserves good pay
4. Professional Conduct ‚Äì Mercenaries, not bandits
5. Reputation Matters ‚Äì Your word is your bond
What This Means:
You're professionals, not murder-hobos
You can refuse any contract before signing
Once you sign, you fulfill it (or face consequences)
You can work for anyone: Thaldros, Aethoria, merchants, nobles, criminals
Your reputation affects future contracts and pay rates

## IRON GUILD REPUTATION SYSTEM

‚úÖ Completing contracts successfully (+2 to +5)

‚úÖ Refusing immoral contracts (+1, but no pay)
‚úÖ Exceptional combat/skill performance (+1)
‚úÖ Saving squad members (+1)
You lose Reputation Points by:
‚ùå Failing contracts (-3 to -10)
‚ùå Betraying employers (-10, possible expulsion)
‚ùå Betraying squad (-20, definite expulsion)
‚ùå Breaking Guild Code (-5 to -15)
‚ùå Cowardice or incompetence (-1 to -5)
Reputation Tiers:
Reputation affects:

Guild support and resources

## SETTING INFORMATION

### THE BORDER REGION
Where You Operate:
The Border is a 50-mile-wide strip of frontier land where the Thaldros Crown's authority fades and independent settlements govern themselves. Neither side fully controls it, making it a haven for:
Mercenaries (like you)
Smugglers and traders
Refugees from both Kingdom and frontier
Bandits and criminals
Independent settlements
The Conflict:
The Thaldros Kingdom wants to bring the frontier under royal control, while the frontier territories fight to remain independent. Neither can afford full-scale war, so they use mercenaries, economic pressure, and political maneuvering. The Border is where this cold war plays out.
The International Angle:
The frontier territories watch this conflict carefully, weighing their options. Some leaders dream of true independence, others seek better terms with the Crown. Rumors say Aethoria across the Sundering Sea watches with interest, perhaps seeing opportunity in Thaldros divided against itself.
Major Border Towns:
Crossroads (Population: 3,000)

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Q: Can I be a good person in this campaign?
A: Yes! Many mercenaries have moral codes. You choose which contracts to take.
Q: Will we be forced to do evil things?
A: No. You can refuse any contract. But refusing loses potential income and some contracts may test your morals.
Q: Can we switch sides during a contract?
A: Technically yes, but expect severe reputation consequences. The Guild Code forbids it.
Q: What if my character wouldn't take a contract?
A: Then don't! But have an in-character reason and accept the consequences.
Q: Can we work for both Kingdom forces and frontier territories?
A: Absolutely. The Guild is neutral. Your only loyalty is to gold and the current contract.
Q: How lethal is this campaign?
A: Moderately lethal. Combat is dangerous but healers are available. Smart play keeps you alive.
Q: Can I play a pacifist?
A: Very difficult in a mercenary campaign. Consider a support role (healer, face) instead.
Q: What if we want to eventually pick a side?
A: That's an option! The campaign can evolve based on your choices.
